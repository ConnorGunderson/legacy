Speaker and Writer

Timeline of Krisha Murti's Life

  • May 11, 1895 - Krishnamurti is born in the small town of Madanapalle, in Madras Presidency (modern-day Chittoor District in Andhra Pradesh).
  • 1909 - Krishnamurti's father is hired by the Theosophical Society in Madras as a clerk and moves there with his family.
  • April 1909 - Krishnamurti is discovered by the Theosophists at Adyar beach.
  • January 11, 1910 - Krishnamurti's first initiation by the Theosophical Society.
  • March 6, 1910 - Krishnamurti adopted by Annie Besant.
  • December 1910 - 'At the Feet of the Master' published.
  • December 28, 1920 - Krishnamurti speaks voluntarily for the first time, at a Theosophical Society meeting.
  • 1922 - Krishnamurti begins to write and speak for The Order of the Star.
  • December 26, 1929 - Krishnamurti resigns from the Theosophical Society. His talks and publications are now organised by the Star Publishing Trust, and later Krishnamurti Writings Inc (KWINC)
  • October 1940 - Between November 1940 and May 1944, during the Second World War, Krishnamurti lived in Ojai, California and did not speak publicly.
  • May 1953 - First publication of Krishnamurti by a commercial publisher: Education and the Significance of Life, published by Harper in the USA and Gollancz in the UK.
  • May 1959 - Commentaries on Living (Second Series) is published.
  • May 1960 - Commentaries on Living (Third Series) is published.
